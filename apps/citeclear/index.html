CiteClear

CiteClear is a citation verification tool that helps legal teams, researchers, and analysts catch citation problems and weak sources before they share a brief, memo, or AI-assisted report.

The page stays intentionally simple: clear problem, clear solution, clear audience, and a privacy-first evaluation story with a direct path into the product instead of a broad keyword expansion around every writing use case.

Problem and audience

  • Primary audience Legal teams, researchers, analysts, consultants
  • Core problem Citation mistakes and weak source quality before a document goes out
  • Workflow stance Local preflight first, optional AI review second

Citation preflight

CiteClear starts by checking citation structure and readiness in the browser so users can catch obvious issues before moving on.

Source-quality review

The product is positioned around stronger source confidence, not just citation formatting, which matters in legal and research-heavy writing.

Optional AI handoff

AI-assisted review exists as a deliberate next step when the user wants deeper help, not as the first thing that happens to their text.

01
Run local preflight checks

Users start with deterministic browser-side review to see whether the citation and source baseline is clean enough to keep going.

02
Review the source-quality issues

CiteClear helps surface where support is thin, unclear, or risky before the document gets shared outward.

03
Choose optional AI review only when needed

The product flow keeps control with the user instead of assuming every document should immediately be sent into an AI workflow.

FAQ

What is CiteClear?

It is a citation verification and source-review tool for legal, research, and AI-assisted writing workflows.

Why does the page talk about local preflight first?

Because that is the product's clearest trust position: check locally first, then only hand off to AI when the user explicitly chooses that step.

Who should evaluate CiteClear?

Legal teams, researchers, analysts, consultants, and anyone producing citation-heavy documents are the clearest fit.
